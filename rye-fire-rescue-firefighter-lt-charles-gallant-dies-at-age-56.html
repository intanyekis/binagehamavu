Rye Fire Rescue firefighter Lt. Charles Gallant dies at age 56

North Hampton, NH –  community is in mourning following the passing of Charles Gallant, a revered figure within the Rye Fire Rescue. His death on Friday, December 15, 2023, has left family, friends, and colleagues in deep sorrow, confirmed through a heartfelt announcement recognizing his two decades of unwavering service to the community.

The exact cause of Charles Gallant’s passing remains undisclosed, intensifying the sense of loss experienced by those who worked alongside him.

Charles, affectionately known as Chuck, served as a dedicated veteran firefighter with Rye Fire Rescue for twenty years. His commitment extended far beyond responding to emergencies in Rye; he dedicated considerable time to community initiatives like the Rye Toy Bank and the MDA, showcasing his unwavering dedication to assisting those in need.

Notably, Chuck held influential roles, including President of the Local 4411 Union, where his leadership fostered a sense of unity among fellow firefighters. His courageous actions were acknowledged with the Paul W. Sypek Medal of Valor in 2014, honoring his extraordinary bravery in the face of danger, a testament to his selfless commitment to public safety.

Chuck’s heroism was evident when he saved a fellow firefighter’s life at the Portsmouth Naval Shipyard, underscoring his selflessness and dedication to serving others.
