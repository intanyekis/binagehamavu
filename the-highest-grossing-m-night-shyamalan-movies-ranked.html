The Highest Grossing M. Night Shyamalan Movies, Ranked

Quick Links

  • After Earth - $243 million
  • Glass  - $247 million
  • Unbreakable - $248 million
  • The Village - $257 million
  • Split - $278 million
  • Avatar: The Last Airbender - $319 million
  • Signs - $408 million
  • The Sixth Sense - $672 million

Night Shyamalan is a well-known director and screenwriter for his intriguing and thrilling films. He was born on August 6, 1970, in Mahé, Pondicherry, India, but raised in Penn Valley, Pennsylvania. His movies frequently have surprising plot twists and surprise endings, making him one of our generation's most creative and innovative filmmakers. Since then, he has made thought-provoking and intriguing films, including Split and Glass, all set in the same universe.

Shyamalan's films are known for their plot twists and surprising endings, and he frequently appears in cameo roles in his movies. Despite mixed reviews for some of his films, Shyamalan has a dedicated fan base and has influenced a generation of filmmakers. We will look at eight of the highest-grossing movies of M. Night Shyamalan's career, according to worldwide box office grosses.

After Earth - $243 million

Will Smith and his son Jaden play a father and son who crashed land on Earth 1,000 years after humankind abandoned it in the science fiction action movie? The movie was directed by Shyamalan, who also collaborated with Gary Whitta on the script. Despite having a well-known cast and a unique premise, After Earth received conflicting reactions from audiences and critics. The fact that it nonetheless managed to gross $243 million globally at the box office proves that Shyamalan's work continues to be popular even when it falls short of the lofty standards that his past hits have set.

Glass  - $247 million

The movie stars James McAvoy, Bruce Willis, and Samuel L. Jackson in key roles and is a sequel to Split and Unbreakable. The film examines the idea of superheroes and villains in the actual world. The movie exemplifies Shyamalan's distinct storytelling approach and capacity to make thought-provoking, compelling movies that hold viewers' attention. The film grossed $247 million at the box office globally despite receiving mixed reviews from critics.

Unbreakable - $248 million

Unbreakable is a psychological thriller film released in 2000, written and directed by M. Night Shyamalan. Bruce Willis plays a security guard who discovers he has superpowers, while Samuel L. Jackson plays a frail man who thinks Willis is a superhero. The movie is praised for Shyamalan's suspenseful thriller and distinct superhero approach. The film grossed $248 million worldwide and became a cult classic, establishing Shyamalan's status as a groundbreaking filmmaker.

The Village - $257 million

The film stars Bryce Dallas Howard, Joaquin Phoenix, and Adrien Brody and follows a group of 19th-century villagers tormented by unknown creatures in the woods. The Village grossed $257 million worldwide for its gripping plot and dramatic finish. Shyamalan's talent for generating evocative and suspenseful stories keeps fans on the edge of their seats throughout the Picture.

Split - $278 million

James McAvoy plays a man with dissociative identity disorder who kidnaps three adolescent girls, while Anya Taylor-Joy plays one of the girls who tries to flee. McAvoy's performance and Shyamalan's directing and story-line were lauded in Split. The film delves into topics of trauma and mental illness, culminating in a surprising twist finale that connects it to Unbreakable. The film grossed $278 million worldwide, making it one of Shyamalan's most commercially and critically successful films.

Avatar: The Last Airbender - $319 million

Avatar: The Last Airbender, a Nickelodeon cartoon series, is adapted into a film about a young boy who can control the elements and defend the world from an evil fire nation. Despite bad reviews and fan backlash, the Last Airbender grossed $319 million worldwide. Shyamalan's ability to adapt rigid source material into complicated, surreal films is shown throughout the film.

Signs - $408 million

Signs is a science fiction horror movie directed by M. Night Shyamalan and released in 2002. The film stars Mel Gibson as Graham Hess, a former reverend who lives with his family on a farm in Pennsylvania. Signs was lauded for its tension, acting, and twisted conclusion. Faith, sadness, and dread of the unknown are explored in this disturbing Picture. The movie grossed $408 million worldwide, establishing Shyamalan as a suspense master and one of Hollywood's most talented directors.

The Sixth Sense - $672 million

Bruce Willis plays a child psychologist who tries to heal a little boy who says he can see ghosts. Haley Joel Osment's career-defining performance in The Sixth Sense and brilliant direction by M. Night Shyamalan helped make it one of the best horror films ever. The movie was an economic and critical success with $672 million worldwide box office revenue and six Academy Award nominations, including Best Picture. Shyamalan's skill as a director is demonstrated by the fact that The Sixth Sense is still regarded as a cultural landmark.
